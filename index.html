<p>Louisiania has coined the nickname "Swamp Country", due to its many southern attractions surrounding its swampland. Not only can tourists visit the highly popular french town of New Orleans, but they can also enjoy many other attractions around the state. If you are daring enough, you may even be able to hold a baby alligator during one of our many alligator tours available! The culture and history surrounding my home state is endless and fascinating. This website will walk you through places, sites, and attractions available to any visitor or habitant. For a little background information, our state flower is the Magnolia, state bird the Pelican, and the name "Louisiana" comes from King Louis and Queen Anna, when they founded the state in 1682.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Above are just some of the many tourist attractions available in Louisiana. For just 56 dollars, you can go to the famous french town of New Orleans for a tour of our swamps. While taking a boat through the swampland, a tour guide will teach you about our state's history. For only 20 dollars, you can go to the small town of Covington, Louisiana, and hold an alligator (at your own risk!). This is a once in a lifetime experience, and i encourage everyone to do it when visiting. In my home city, Baton Rouge, you can see the beloved mascot of SEC's college football team LSU. Mike the Tiger stays in a glass habitat, and loves visitors.</p>

<p><strong>While everyone is bias to their own state, I believe Louisiana has the most tourist-pleasing attractions for 3 reasons:</strong></p>
